Nutrition

Why Nutrition Is Crucial

Nutrition is the foundation of your fitness journey. No matter how hard you train, your body needs the right fuel to build muscle, burn fat, recover properly, and perform at its best. Just like a car can’t run without quality fuel, your body can’t reach peak performance without proper nutrition.

Aligning Diet with Fitness Goals

Your diet should match your goal — whether that’s building muscle, losing fat, or maintaining overall health.
For example: To build muscle, you need a calorie surplus with enough protein. To lose fat, you need a calorie deficit while preserving lean mass. For performance and endurance, balanced meals with the right carbs and hydration are key. Eating the right foods in the right amounts makes your workouts more effective and helps you see real, lasting results.

Macronutrients Breakdown

Let's take a look at the breakdown of macronutrients.
Macronutrients are the three main nutrients your body needs in large amounts to function properly and fuel your workouts.

Protein

Role: Builds and repairs muscles, supports recovery, and helps maintain lean mass.
Sources: Chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, tofu, lentils, protein shakes.
General Target: 1.6–2.2g of protein per kg of body weight (for active individuals).


Carbohydrates

Role: Your body’s primary energy source, especially important for intense training and recovery.
Sources: Rice, oats, potatoes, fruits, vegetables, whole grains.
Types:

  • Complex carbs: Slow-digesting, steady energy (e.g., brown rice, quinoa)
  • Simple carbs: Quick energy (e.g., fruits, white bread)

Fats

Role: Supports hormone production, brain function, and overall health.
Sources: Avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish, seeds.
Types:

  • Healthy fats: Unsaturated fats (good for heart and hormones)
  • Unhealthy fats: Trans fats and excess saturated fats (limit these)
